On the Subject of Rubik's Clock

Time is a relative concept. But it can absolutely turn you crazy.

Solve it like an actual Rubik's Clock.
https://www.google.com/search?q=how+to+solve+the+rubiks+clock

Alternatively:

  • Select an instruction in the table below based on which clock is lit (big square) and which pin is lit (small square).
  • Select a modification and an amount from the second table.
    First time: The first and second character of the serial number determine the first modification and amount.
    Next times: Select the next modification and amount. Bottom loops back to top.
  • Apply the modification. The instruction table is cyclic; if you get out of bounds, continue on the opposite side.
  • Execute the modified instruction. The top two arrows of the instruction indicate which pins to change (pull or push, depending on their current state). The bottom arrow and number indicate which gear to rotate for how many hours. Prime' means counterclockwise.
  • Now turn the Clock over to the other side.
  • Repeat until all clocks, front and back, are set to 12 o'clock.
